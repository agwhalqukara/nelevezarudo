What Is David Sedaris Net Worth In 2022?

What Is David Sedaris’ Net Worth In 2022? David Raymond Sedaris is an American comedian, author, radio host, and humorist. In 1992, when National Public Radio played his essay “Santaland Diaries,” he became known to the public.

His first collection of essays and short stories, Barrel Fever, came out in 1994. His next book, Naked, which came out in 1997, was the first of a string of New York Times bestsellers, and his collection of short stories, Me Talk Pretty One Day, which came out in 2000, won the Thurber Prize for American Humor.

Here’s everything you need to know about David Sedaris net worth and career.

David Sedaris’ Net Worth

According to Celebrity Net Worth, David Sedaris has an estimated net worth of $8 million. David Sedaris was born in Binghamton, New York, in 1956. He grew up outside of Raleigh, North Carolina, and went to Western Carolina University and Kent State University before graduating from the School of the Art Institute of Chicago in the late 1980s.

Know About David Sedaris Career

While Sedaris was working odd jobs in Raleigh, Chicago, and New York City, radio host Ira Glass met him in a club in Chicago. Sedaris was reading a diary that he had kept since 1977. Glass was so impressed with his work that he asked him to appear on his weekly local show, The Wild Room. Referring to the chance, Sedaris said, “I owe everything I have to Ira… My life just changed completely, as if someone waved a magic wand.” Sedaris’s success on The Wild Room led to his first appearance on National Public Radio on December 23, 1992, when he read a radio essay on Morning Edition titled “Santaland Diaries,” in which he wrote about his supposed experiences as an elf at Macy’s department store in New York during Christmas.

“Santaland Diaries” was a hit with listeners and made Sedaris what The New York Times called “a minor event.” He started recording a monthly segment for NPR that was based on his diary entries and was edited and produced by Glass. He also signed a two-book deal with Little, Brown, and Company. Sedaris told The New York Times in 1993 that he was publishing his first book, a collection of stories and essays, and that he had 70 pages written of his second book, a novel “about a man who keeps a diary and whom Mr. Sedaris described as “not me, but a lot like me.”

Personal Life

Sedaris lives in Rackham, West Sussex, England, with his longtime partner, the painter and set designer Hugh Hamrick. Sedaris mentions Hamrick in a number of his stories and says that the two of them are “the kind of couple who wouldn’t get married.”

Sedaris is known to frequently wear a headlamp at night and spend hours picking up trash on roads and highways near Rackham. Because of this hobby, people in his town call him “Pig Pen,” and a garbage truck is named after him.
