Early life and education

Commonly known as Ella, the domestic worker from Abo Mzala, Salamina Mosese is a very educated and intelligent woman. She matriculated with distinctions in English, Afrikaans, and History. She enrolled in the University of Johannesburg, where she studied Corporate Communications.

Born in Zebediela, a small village in Limpopo, the famous actress landed an acting gig entirely by chance. She had taken her brother to try out for a role, she was encouraged to audition, and landed a gig. Since then, it has been an upward trajectory for the gifted actress who bodies every acting opportunity she gets, even those out of her comfort zone.

Successful career

She has been in the TV industry for more than 20 years. Her first job was as a presenter for CRAZe, an eTV kids program, where she stayed for 5 years. Around that time, she acted in Soul Buddyz, a youth-centred show by SABC1.

In 2007, she presented Crazy Games on SABC2 and at the same time landed a gig presenting on the reality show Top Class on SABC1. She started making major waves in the industry in 2008 when she won Top Billing presenter search, and in that same year, she began presenting that same show. She was also the presenter of two Nedbank gameshows on SABC3 and eTV.

Another major role she has worked on is Ella, a domestic worker who is romantic at heart and waiting for her prince charming who will save her from her miseries. Her hilarious performance earned her a SAFTA for the Best actress in a comedy. The show AboMzala revealed a comical side to the talented actress.

Health struggles

So far, Salamina's life looks like a bed of roses, but this is far from the case. She has had her fair share of problems. She uses her experiences to motivate and advise others.
